# Khaya — Go-to-Market Strategy

## The wedge: start narrow, own "trust"
Don't fight SweepSouth on cleaning. Win the niche nobody owns:
**permanent, deeply-vetted placement of people who enter your home & care for your family**
— starting with **nannies + elderly care in Johannesburg** (Sandton, Bryanston, Midrand, Pretoria).
These are the highest-trust, highest-anxiety, highest-willingness-to-pay categories.

## Who we beat, and how
| Competitor | Their lane | How Khaya wins |
|---|---|---|
| SweepSouth / Domestly | on-demand cleaning, light vetting | deeper vetting, permanent placement, all categories |
| Maid4U / Domestic Angels / NannyMaids | offline agencies, opaque, single-city | transparent profiles, ratings, tech, contracts you can see |
| Word-of-mouth / Facebook groups | free but risky, no checks | actual criminal/ID/reference verification + a guarantee |

## The flywheel (defensible moat)
Treat workers with dignity → best workers join Khaya → better supply →
employers get better matches → more employers → more work for workers → repeat.
Workers rating employers (not just the reverse) is the unlock SweepSouth's model resists.

## Launch plan (first 90 days)
1. **Waitlist now.** This site (free on GitHub Pages) collects employer + worker signups.
   Goal: 100 employers + 200 vetted-ready workers before full launch.
2. **Seed supply first.** Recruit + vet 50 nannies/carers via existing domestic-worker
   WhatsApp groups, churches, training colleges. Supply is the hard side — get it ahead of demand.
3. **Demand via trust-anxiety channels:**
   - Facebook parenting groups (Joburg Moms, Sandton Families) — *organic posts, not ads, first.*
   - School & estate newsletters / WhatsApp groups (HOAs love a vetted-help angle).
   - Instagram: short reels — "How we vet every Khaya nanny" (transparency = marketing).
4. **Content = the vetting process.** Show the checks. Trust is sold by *showing the receipts*:
   a 30-sec reel of the SAPS check, the reference call, the badge. This is free and on-brand.
5. **Referral loop:** employers refer employers (free placement credit); workers refer vetted workers.

## Channels ranked (SA reality)
1. **WhatsApp** — worker side lives here. Build intake + updates on WhatsApp.
2. **Facebook Groups** — where anxious parents already ask "anyone know a trusted nanny?"
3. **Instagram Reels / TikTok** — the vetting-transparency content.
4. **Estate / school partnerships** — B2B2C trust transfer.
5. **Google Search** ("vetted nanny Johannesburg") — high intent, do SEO + later ads.

## Pricing (validate, don't assume)
- **Workers: free** to join and get vetted (charge only pass-through cost of the criminal check).
- **Employers: placement fee per successful hire** (e.g. R1,500–3,500 by category/tier) +
  optional subscription for frequent hirers. **Replacement guarantee** is the trust closer.

## Metrics that matter
- Vetted-worker supply count (the bottleneck), time-to-first-placement,
  employer repeat rate, % placements with signed BCEA contract, NPS, zero safety incidents.

## The one risk to manage from day one
A single bad placement can kill a trust brand. **The vetting must be real, contracts must cap
liability, and carry insurance / a guarantee.** Trust is the product — protect it like the asset it is.
